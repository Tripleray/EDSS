How to Use

  • 1.Access website, 2.Plug in attributes of water system into interface
  • 3.System attributes results, 4.Establish RFID with client
  • 5. Professional Review
  • Fo realz this is what itz bout

Herp Derp:

1.Access website: Individuals who are considering using floating treatment wetlands access a website created specifically to help establish the parameters of the water system they want to treat. This website would be equipped with a user friendly interface that gives the user step by step instructions on how to input the parameters required for the initial process of implementation.

2.Plug in attributes of water system into interface: First, users would access the website and click a button that would allow them to start the process to discover the possibilities their body of water has with floating treatment wetland\ implementation. Once they are in Google Earth users would input the parameters required by following the instructions given on the website. These parameters include surface area of the water body (ft2), average depth (ft), inflow and outflow locations, as well as an option to overlay a topographical map acquired through website instructions for Google Earth. The topographical map will help in assessing where runoff comes from and enters the users body of water. This may be important because it will help the system better understand whether or not excess nutrients are entering their system as inorganic fertilizers or organic fertilizers.

The Google Earth information is valuable information that would allow professionals to acquire any further information needed regarding the water system. This would include calculating the surface area of your water body through the Google Earth polygon formation tool in conjunction with a tool that calculates the surface area from that polygon file. Currently earthpoint.us is a website that has that functionality and may be considered for this technology, but it would be smoother to have a tool already attached to the main EDSS website.

Derp Herp:

3.System attributes results: After users input all parameters required, the website would output a diagram with all data attributes required for a system including surface area of the water body (ft2), average depth (ft), inflow and outflow locations, and a topographical map. This information will be sent to both the user and the professionals responsible for implementation. In the beginning, if this technology were to be created, professional labs would take samples from clients for reading phosphorous concentration values until there was enough data in the database to allow for accurate assumptions to be made. These assumptions made by the system, or trained inspectors of each case, would then decide what volume/surface area of FTWs are required to reach the goals of the client based on case studies and collected data from various sources. This allows for a dynamic system that is constantly evolving.

4.Establish RFID with client: By sending this to both parties, users will have a template standard of day one for future reference and professionals can establish a reference ID number with the user.

Sup fam?

5. Professional Review: After all information has been gathered, professionals will be equipped with enough knowledge to determine if floating treatment wetlands are a viable solution to the users problem. Professional FTW companies can then schedule meetings for discussion of solutions, implementation, and management of the FTWs with the user. This system allows for zero capital investment from the user for a simplified answer as to how to best use FTWs, as well as schedule a more detailed and thorough site investigation by trained professionals.

Fo realz this is what itz bout

Floating Treatment Wetlands if used and managed properly have the potential to solve many issues: mitigating contaminant pollution of various types, increase bank/shoreline stabilization through wave power absorption, create floating docks to provide wildlife and people with a healthy ecosystem that is productive, stormwater/wastewater remediation, wastewater treatment, and so on. Because of these reasons we believe that the public deserves an easy way to access the information about the various applications of Floating Treatment Wetland technology and the impact it can have on the environment that surrounds us. In order to achieve this goal, we propose to create an integrated virtual “middle-man” between the Floating Treatment Wetland contractors and distributors, and the public; whether that be large environmental consultant agencies or average citizens with a pond that has too many algae blooms to be a successful ecosystem. That is why there are two basic components of our proposed system: a free easy to use informative website, and a more integrated system focused on action involving Floating Treatment Wetland contractors that is for profit.
